# What is Forex (FX)?

**Foreign Exchange**, commonly called **Forex** or **FX**, is the global market where one currency is exchanged for another.

It is the **largest and most liquid financial market** in the world, with an average daily trading volume in the **trillions of dollars**. Forex operates **24 hours a day**, 5 days a week, and includes all currencies of the world.

Forex is also referred to as:
- **FOREX**
- **FX**
- **Spot FX**

---

## How Forex Trading Works

Forex traders **exchange two different currencies** and aim to profit from the **price difference** between the buy and sell prices.

Since currency values are constantly fluctuating, traders can earn profits by buying low and selling high at the right time.

### Example:
When the **US Dollar** is expected to weaken against the **Euro**, traders **sell USD** and **buy EUR**.

If the Euro actually appreciates, traders can **sell EUR** and **buy back more USD** than they initially had, making a **profit**.

---

## Currency Pairs

Currencies are always quoted in **pairs**, such as:
- **EUR/USD**
- **USD/JPY**
- **GBP/USD**

- The **first currency** is called the **Base Currency**
- The **second currency** is called the **Quote Currency** (or Counter Currency)

The exchange rate tells you **how much of the quote currency** is needed to buy **one unit** of the base currency.

---

## Exchange Rate

The **exchange rate** is the price of one currency in terms of another.

### Example:
**USD/JPY Exchange Rate**

## What is a PIP?

**PIP** stands for **Point In Percentage**. It is the smallest unit of price movement in Forex.

- For most currency pairs: **1 PIP = 0.0001** (4th decimal place)
- For Japanese Yen pairs (e.g., USD/JPY): **1 PIP = 0.01** (2nd decimal place)

### Examples:
- **EUR/USD** rises from **1.16997** to **1.17007** → **+1 PIP**
- **USD/JPY** drops from **110.200** to **110.145** → **-5.5 PIPs**

---

## Spread

The **spread** is the difference between the **Buy (Ask)** price and the **Sell (Bid)** price of a currency pair.

**Example:**
- EUR/USD Sell price: **1.4079**
- EUR/USD Buy price: **1.4081**
- **Spread = 2 pips**

---

## Bid Price & Ask (Offer) Price

- **Bid Price**: The price at which you can **sell** the base currency.
- **Ask Price**: The price at which you can **buy** the base currency.

**Example:**
- USD/JPY quoted as **113.67 / 113.69**
  - **Bid (113.67)** → You can sell 1 USD for 113.67 JPY
  - **Ask (113.69)** → You can buy 1 USD for 113.69 JPY

The difference between Bid and Ask is the **Spread**.

---

## Leverage

Leverage allows traders to control a **large position** with a **small amount of capital**.

**Example:**
- With **$200** in your account and **1:500 leverage**, you can control up to **$100,000** worth of currency.

---

## Margin

**Margin** is the amount of money required in your trading account to open a position.

- **Free Margin** = Available money that can be used to open new trades.

**Formula:**
Margin = (Current Market Price × Lot Size × Volume) / Leverage
text---

## Rollover (Swap)

**Rollover** or **Swap** is the interest earned or paid when a position is held overnight.

It occurs due to the **interest rate differential** between the two currencies in the pair.
